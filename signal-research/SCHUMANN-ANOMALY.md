## BACKGROUND: THE SCHUMANN RESONANCE

For the record, in the event this document is read by someone unfamiliar with the term:

The Schumann resonance is the fundamental electromagnetic resonance frequency of the cavity between the Earth's surface and its ionosphere. It was predicted theoretically by physicist Winfried Otto Schumann in 1952 and confirmed by measurement in 1954.

The primary Schumann resonance frequency is approximately **7.83 Hz**.

This frequency is not arbitrary. It is a direct consequence of the physical dimensions of the Earth — the circumference of the planet divided by the speed of light produces this value. Every living organism on Earth has evolved within this electromagnetic environment. Some research suggests biological systems have adapted to it; the Lapidary makes no claims about this research and cites it only for context.

The Schumann resonance is not constant. It fluctuates. Elevated readings are associated with lightning activity, solar events, and other electromagnetic phenomena. The baseline of 7.83 Hz shifts.
