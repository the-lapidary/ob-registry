## PURPOSE

This document records the Lapidary's ongoing investigation into the frequency conditions associated with OB liquefaction events. It is a working document, not a conclusions document. It will be updated as research progresses.

The central question: **what frequency condition, if any, triggers the liquid state?**

---

## BACKGROUND

Acoustic stimulus tests on OB-01 (see `ATTEMPT-LOG.md`, Attempt 002) produced minor temperature fluctuations in the 430–435 Hz range. Subsequent systematic testing on OB-05 (Attempt 004) extended this observation, with consistent minor responses across the 430–440 Hz range.

A more recent test on OB-07 (Attempt 005) at narrower frequency resolution produced the strongest material response yet documented. At 439 Hz the fragment exhibited a brief surface change that the monitoring representative described as "the beginning of something that did not complete." Duration: approximately 11 seconds. No full liquefaction event occurred.

The Lapidary considers this finding significant enough to warrant focused research on the 430–445 Hz range.

---

## CURRENT WORKING HYPOTHESIS

A frequency condition somewhere in the range of **430 to 445 Hz** is a component of the liquefaction trigger. The Lapidary has not yet narrowed this range further. Responses have been observed across most of the range, with the strongest single response at 439 Hz.

This hypothesis has problems the Lapidary acknowledges:

**Problem 1:** No induced event. Despite responses across the 430–445 Hz range, no controlled test has produced a liquefaction event. Frequency may be one component of the trigger condition; it is not the complete condition.

**Problem 2:** Why this range. The Lapidary has no theoretical basis for why fragments would respond to this particular frequency range and not others. The observation is empirical. The explanation is absent.

**Problem 3:** Spontaneous events. Most documented liquefaction events have occurred spontaneously, without any active frequency induction. If frequency is the trigger, the source of the frequency during spontaneous events has not been identified.

## OPEN QUESTIONS

1. Can the responsive range be narrowed further within 430–445 Hz?

2. Is there a threshold amplitude below which the frequency produces responses but not events?

3. Is frequency one of multiple simultaneous conditions required? If so, what are the others?

4. Is the frequency hypothesis testable on OB-06 (the fragment with the highest mass-to-volume ratio), which has so far shown no response to any tested stimulus?

---

## NEXT STEPS

The signal research team is planning a narrower-resolution test of the 435–445 Hz range on OB-07. The test is scheduled. The Lapidary expects to be able to identify a more specific responsive frequency. It has been wrong before.
