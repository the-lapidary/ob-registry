## PURPOSE

This document records the Lapidary's ongoing investigation into the frequency conditions associated with OB liquefaction events. It is a working document, not a conclusions document. It will be updated as research progresses.

The central question: **what frequency condition, if any, triggers the liquid state?**

---

## BACKGROUND: WHY FREQUENCY

During OB-07's third liquefaction event (FIELD-REPORT-0089, Event 3), a Lapidary representative was present and conducting a frequency induction test at the time of onset. The test involved generating tonal frequencies in the range of 400–500 Hz at low amplitude in proximity to the fragment.

The event began while the representative was testing at approximately 441 Hz.

The Lapidary does not conclude from this that 441 Hz caused the event. The representative had been cycling through frequencies for approximately 40 minutes before the event began. The event may have been spontaneous and the frequency coincidental.

The Lapidary does conclude from this that frequency is worth investigating.

---

## METHODOLOGY

Controlled frequency induction tests have been conducted on OB-01, OB-03, OB-05, and OB-07. Tests on other registered fragments have not been possible due to access limitations.

Test protocol:
- Fragment isolated in temperature-controlled environment
- Frequencies generated using a calibrated signal generator and full-range speaker array
- Range: 20 Hz – 20,000 Hz, tested in discrete steps
- Amplitude: low (approximately 60dB at 1 metre) to avoid confounding vibration effects
- Duration at each frequency: 5 minutes
- Monitoring: temperature, mass readings, surface optical properties, instrumental

Results recorded here are observations only. No event has been induced.

## FINDINGS BY FREQUENCY RANGE

### Below 200 Hz
No fragment has shown response to frequencies below 200 Hz in controlled conditions. This does not exclude low-frequency conditions occurring naturally at time of spontaneous events. The Lapidary has not been able to monitor ambient low-frequency conditions at fragment locations in most cases.

### 200–400 Hz
No consistent fragment response. Some minor temperature fluctuations observed during tests in the 350–380 Hz range on OB-07, within the range of monitoring noise. Not statistically significant.

### 400–450 Hz — PRIMARY RESEARCH RANGE

This range is where the Lapidary's attention is focused.

**410–430 Hz:** Minor responses observed in OB-07 and OB-05. Surface optical properties showed brief increases in what the Lapidary is calling "depth apparent" — a quality difficult to quantify precisely, in which the surface appears momentarily to recede rather than reflect. Duration: seconds. Not consistent across trials.

**430–435 Hz:** Responses become more consistent in OB-07. Temperature fluctuations exceed monitoring noise. The "depth apparent" quality more pronounced. Duration: up to 30 seconds per trial. No liquefaction event induced.

**435–440 Hz:** Strongest consistent responses observed. OB-07 trials at 438, 439, and 440 Hz produced:
- Temperature drops of 0.4°C–0.8°C during the test period
- "Depth apparent" quality sustained throughout the test period (5 minutes)
- In one trial at 439 Hz: a brief surface change lasting approximately 11 seconds that the monitoring representative described as "the beginning of something." No full transition occurred.

**440–445 Hz:** Responses diminish above 440 Hz. By 443 Hz, responses return to below the threshold of statistical significance. By 445 Hz, no detectable response.

**Above 450 Hz:** No response in any fragment tested.

## CURRENT WORKING HYPOTHESIS

The Lapidary's current hypothesis: a frequency condition in the range of **438–440 Hz** is a component of the liquefaction trigger.

This hypothesis has problems the Lapidary acknowledges:

**Problem 1:** No induced event. Despite strong responses in the 438–440 Hz range, no controlled test has produced a liquefaction event. This means either: (a) frequency alone is insufficient and additional conditions are required, (b) the amplitude used in controlled tests is insufficient, or (c) the frequency range identified is a correlate of the trigger condition rather than the trigger itself.

**Problem 2:** The 440 Hz standard. 440 Hz is the international standard concert pitch (A4 = 440 Hz, established as the ISO standard in 1955). The Lapidary is aware that the frequency showing the strongest material response in controlled conditions is also the most common sustained frequency in the modern built environment — present in any space with electrical equipment, HVAC systems, or ambient music. This creates a confound: if 440 Hz is the trigger condition, or part of it, then fragments are being exposed to it constantly and events should be more frequent. They are not. The Lapidary does not have a resolution for this confound.

**Problem 3:** Spontaneous events. Most liquefaction events have occurred spontaneously, without any active frequency induction. If frequency is the trigger, the source of the frequency during spontaneous events has not been identified.

## OPEN QUESTIONS

1. What is the relationship between the 438–440 Hz response range and the spontaneous event trigger?

2. Is there a threshold amplitude below which the frequency produces responses but not events? If so, what is the threshold?

3. Is frequency one of multiple simultaneous conditions required? If so, what are the others?

4. Why does OB-06 — the fragment with the highest mass-to-volume ratio — show no response to any tested frequency?

5. The Schumann resonance anomaly (see `SCHUMANN-ANOMALY.md`) operates at 7.83 Hz, a fundamentally different range. The relationship between 7.83 Hz and 438–440 Hz has not been modelled. Should it be?
