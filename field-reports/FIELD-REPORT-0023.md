## HOLDER'S ACCOUNT

*Submitted as written statement. Lightly formatted for registry. Content unaltered.*

I have had the fragment for eleven years. In eleven years it has never done anything other than be what it is — very heavy, very cold, and completely incapable of being described to anyone who hasn't seen it.

On the evening in question I was in the room with the fragment and one other person. We were not doing anything in particular with the fragment. We were in the same room as it. This is something I do occasionally, for reasons I haven't been able to fully articulate to myself or anyone else.

The other person said: "is it doing something."

I looked at it. It was.

The surface was no longer the way the surface is. The surface had become the way water is when there is no light — not reflective, not moving, but evidently liquid. The absolute absence of reflectance was the same. But it was not solid.

We stood at the edge of the room and watched it for the duration. Neither of us approached. I don't know if this was caution or something else. We watched it for what I estimated as forty to fifty seconds. Then it was solid again. The temperature in the room was noticeably lower. We stood there for a while after. Then we left the room.

I have been trying to write this account for several weeks. This is the account I am able to give.

## LAPIDARY REPRESENTATIVE'S NOTES

*Appended following conversation with holder and second witness.*

The second witness's account is consistent with the holder's in all material respects. The second witness added one detail the holder did not mention: during the event, the depth of the liquid surface appeared to exceed the physical dimensions of the fragment. The fragment is 7cm at its deepest point. The surface, in the liquid state, appeared to have no bottom that either witness could see.

Neither witness approached. Neither lowered anything into the surface.

The Lapidary notes this is the first documented event in the registry. The Lapidary notes that the first documented event in the registry involved two witnesses who stood at the edge of the room. The Lapidary does not judge this. The Lapidary records it.
